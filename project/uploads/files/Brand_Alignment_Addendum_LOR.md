# Engineered Safety Platform
## Brand Alignment Addendum – Laing O'Rourke Visual Identity Integration
### v1.0 | April 2026

---

## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

This document specifies how the Engineered Safety Platform incorporates Laing O'Rourke's visual identity, as defined in the **Visual Identity Guidelines v7.1 (2022)**.

The platform embraces LOR's brand philosophy of **"Sophisticated Simplicity"** — combining the company's bold, distinctive visual language with clean, accessible user interfaces optimized for enterprise construction workflows.

## 2. TYPOGRAPHY ALIGNMENT

The Engineered Safety Platform uses Laing O'Rourke's corporate typeface standards:

### 2.1 Primary Typeface: Century Gothic

**Usage**: All user-facing text, forms, buttons, and system messaging.

**Font Weights & Sizes**:

| Level | Size (Desktop) | Size (Mobile) | Weight | Application |
|-------|---|---|---|---|
| **H1** | 32px | 24px | Bold (700) | Page titles, hero sections |
| **H2** | 24px | 20px | Semibold (600) | Section headers, card titles |
| **H3** | 18px | 16px | Semibold (600) | Subsections, form section titles |
| **Body** | 14px | 14px | Regular (400) | Main text, descriptions |
| **Small** | 12px | 12px | Regular (400) | Labels, helper text, captions |
| **Button** | 14px | 14px | Semibold (600) | All button text |

### 2.2 Secondary Typeface: Ginger (Professional Designers Only)

**Critical Constraint**: Staff members of Laing O'Rourke **cannot use Ginger** in any documents or interfaces without express permission from Corporate Affairs (comms@laingorourke.com).

Only professional graphic designers working on behalf of Laing O'Rourke may use Ginger, and only after purchasing a license from F37 Foundry: https://f37foundry.com/font-library/f37-ginger/

**Recommendation for Platform**: Do NOT use Ginger in the platform. Century Gothic is sufficient and aligns with internal communication standards.

### 2.3 Capitalization Rules

Per LOR guidelines:
- **Capitals**: Reserved for document covers, section dividers, and key headings. Do NOT use for long copy.
- **Sentence Case**: Use for body text, form labels, buttons, and regular paragraphs.
- **Titles**: Use Title Case for section headers and major headings.

**Example (Correct)**:
```
SUBMIT NEW IDEA
Quickly log your safety innovation with supporting details
[Submit Button]
```

**Example (Incorrect)**:
```
SUBMIT NEW IDEA
QUICKLY LOG YOUR SAFETY INNOVATION WITH SUPPORTING DETAILS
[SUBMIT BUTTON]
```

## 3. LOGO & MOTIF USAGE IN PLATFORM

### 3.1 Logo Placement & Sizing

**Header Logo**:
- **Position**: Top-left corner of header (per LOR guidelines)
- **Height**: 20mm on A4 documents; scale proportionally for screen (typically 32–48px height on desktop, 24–32px on mobile)
- **Clear Space**: Maintain 3× the height of the "K" character in the logo as margin on all sides (per LOR clear space rules)
- **Background**: White or light grey (#f2f2f2) background. Never place on black or dark backgrounds without special treatment (spot varnish or animation effects for web, not applicable to digital platform)

**Favicon**:
- Use the simplified LOR logo (black box element only) at 16×16px and 32×32px
- Maintains recognition while fitting small spaces

### 3.2 Motif Usage (Limited)

The LOR **motif** (the three colored bars) is **NOT** a substitute for the full logo and should NOT be used independently in the platform interface.

**Acceptable Use Cases**:
- As part of the footer address block (with contact info and tagline)
- In the tagline: "THE POWER OF EXPERIENCE" (appears as pre-designed Adobe Illustrator asset)

**Prohibited Use Cases**:
- As a decorative element elsewhere in the UI
- As a replacement for the logo
- Recolored or modified in any way

### 3.3 Logo Variants

Use the **full-color primary logo** in all circumstances. 

**Secondary/Reversed logos** (monochrome, reversed) should only be used if:
1. The platform cannot support full color in a specific context (rare), AND
2. The designer has confirmed with Corporate Affairs (comms@laingorourke.com) that this is necessary

## 5. PHOTOGRAPHY & IMAGERY GUIDELINES

### 5.1 Photo Style

The Engineered Safety Platform should feature Laing O'Rourke photography from three core themes:

| Theme | Application in Platform | Example Content |
|-------|---------|-------|
| **Projects** | Hero images, case studies, solution galleries | Completed structures, construction phases, project milestones |
| **People** | User testimonials, team features, safety culture | Diverse workforce, apprentices, safety practices, team collaboration |
| **Innovation & Engineering** | Technical solutions, process flows, DfMA examples | Offsite manufacturing, precast installation, digital engineering models |

### 5.2 Image Quality Standards

- **Resolution**: High-quality, sharp images suitable for web and print
- **Composition**: Striking, carefully composed (portrait-style for projects)
- **Consistency**: All imagery should reflect LOR's professional, modern approach
- **Diversity**: Feature a diverse, inclusive workforce representing LOR's global operations

### 5.3 Color vs. Black & White

**Corporate/Board-Level Materials** (dashboards, executive reports):
- Use **black & white or greyscale imagery** paired with **secondary colours** for highlights
- Creates professional, analytical tone

**Marketing Materials** (external solution gallery, case studies):
- Use **full-color imagery** with more vibrant secondary colours
- Engages external audiences

**Note**: Do NOT mix colour and black & white within the same document or page.

### 5.4 Image Access

Access Laing O'Rourke photography library at: **www.lorimedia.co.uk**
- Register to access high-resolution images
- All images comply with brand guidelines and usage rights

## 6. DOCUMENT GRIDS & LAYOUT STANDARDS

### 6.1 Grid Systems for Exported Documents

If the platform exports to PDF or Word documents, use LOR's document grid systems:

**Externally Printed (A4)**:
- 8-column grid with 4mm gutters
- 12mm margins on all sides

**Internally Printed (A4)** (if printed on LOR photocopiers):
- 12-column grid with 4mm gutters
- 12mm margins (accounting for photocopier limits)

**A3 & Larger**:
- Scale the above proportionally
- 20mm margins for both internal and external print

### 6.2 Spacing & Margins

Per LOR guidelines, use consistent spacing:

- **Page margins**: 12–20mm (interior: 12–16mm, exterior: 16–20mm)
- **Section spacing**: 24–32px
- **Element spacing**: 16px
- **Gutter width**: 4mm (print) / 4px (screen)

## 7. GRAPHIC & TYPOGRAPHICAL ELEMENTS

### 7.1 Decorative Elements (Per LOR Guidelines)

When designing cards, panels, or sections within the platform, consider these LOR-approved techniques:

| Element | Description | Usage in Platform |
|---------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Underline rules** | Solid lines, 2–4px height, in primary colors (red, yellow, teal) | Emphasize section headers, dividers, key information |
| **Tint panels** | Coloured backgrounds (light tints of secondary colours) | Highlight case studies, related information, warnings |
| **Pull-out quotes** | Quoted text in tinted panel with borders between items | Testimonials, key insights, safety messages |
| **Introductory paragraphs** | Bolded, 2pts larger than body text | Lead paragraphs on cards, case studies, highlights |
| **Graphic underlines** | Heavy rules (8–12px) at page footer/header | Link pages of multi-page exports, balance composition |

**Colour palette for panels**:
- Use **tints** of secondary colours (Teal, Green, Orange) at 25%, 55%, or 80% opacity
- Light tints create visual hierarchy without overwhelming text
- Ensure text contrast remains WCAG AA compliant (4.5:1)

## 11. ACCESSIBILITY & BRAND COMPLIANCE

### 11.1 Contrast Requirements

All text and interactive elements must meet **WCAG 2.1 AA** minimum standards (4.5:1 contrast ratio for normal text, 3:1 for large text).

**Compliant Text Color Pairings**:

| Text Color | Background | Contrast Ratio | Status |
|------------|-----------|-----------------|--------|
| #2d2d2d (Almost Black) | #f2f2f2 (Light Grey) | 15.7:1 | ✓ Excellent |
| #4d4d4d (Very Dark Grey) | #ffffff (White) | 8.6:1 | ✓ Good |
| #e30613 (Red) | #ffffff (White) | 5.2:1 | ✓ Good |
| #00a7b5 (Teal) | #ffffff (White) | 6.8:1 | ✓ Good |
| #64a70b (Green) | #ffffff (White) | 7.9:1 | ✓ Good |

**Non-Compliant Pairings** (Avoid):
- Yellow (#fff200) on white background (1.1:1) ✗
- Yellow (#fff200) on light grey (too low) ✗
- Dark grey on dark grey (insufficient contrast) ✗

### 11.2 Colorblind-Safe Design

Per LOR guidelines, **never convey information using color alone**. Always pair with:
- **Icons** (checkmarks, X, arrows)
- **Text labels** (green = "Approved", red = "Rejected")
- **Patterns** (stripes, solids, gradients)
- **Position** (grouped together)

**Example (Correct)**:
```
✓ Approved (green badge + checkmark + text)
⚠ At Risk (orange badge + clock icon + "7 days pending")
✗ Rejected (red badge + X icon + text)
```

**Example (Incorrect)**:
```
Approved (just green box, no other indicator)
At Risk (just orange box, no icon)
Rejected (just red box, no X)
```

## 12. BRAND COMPLIANCE CHECKLIST

Use this checklist when reviewing platform designs:

### Color & Styling
- [ ] Primary actions use LOR Red (#e30613) or Teal (#00a7b5)
- [ ] Neutral elements use LOR greys (#f2f2f2 to #2d2d2d)
- [ ] All text uses Century Gothic (NOT Ginger for internal use)
- [ ] Logo positioned top-left with proper clear space
- [ ] No modifications to logo colors, proportions, or elements
- [ ] Decorative elements (rules, tints, underlines) use LOR-approved colors

### Accessibility
- [ ] All text meets WCAG AA contrast (4.5:1 minimum)
- [ ] Status information conveyed with icons + labels, not color alone
- [ ] Interactive elements have visible focus states (3px outline in Teal)
- [ ] Form labels present (not hidden in placeholders)
- [ ] Touch targets minimum 44×44px

### Typography
- [ ] Headings: Century Gothic Semibold/Bold, proper sizing scale
- [ ] Body text: Century Gothic Regular, 14px+, line-height 1.5
- [ ] No long paragraphs in ALL CAPS
- [ ] Capitals reserved for headers, dividers, covers

### Photography & Imagery
- [ ] Images are high-quality, sharp, professional
- [ ] Diverse workforce shown in people photos
- [ ] Projects and innovation themes well-represented
- [ ] Consistent colour/black-and-white usage (not mixed)
- [ ] All people in photos have proper consent & permissions (GDPR)

### Documentation & Exports
- [ ] Tagline "THE POWER OF EXPERIENCE" appears on final documents
- [ ] Logo + contact address block included in footers
- [ ] Document grids follow LOR spacing (12mm–20mm margins)
- [ ] Exported PDFs use 72 dpi for online, 300 dpi for print

### Video & Multimedia
- [ ] Video intros/outros use branded templates
- [ ] Logo appears in all videos (intro & outro)
- [ ] 16:9 aspect ratio maintained
- [ ] Social icons included in external-facing videos
- [ ] Tagline displayed in final frame
